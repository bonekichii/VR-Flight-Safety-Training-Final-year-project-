	\Large \textbf{ABSTRACT}
\end{center}
\vspace{0.5cm}

Current pre-flight safety briefings, whether conducted manually by crew or via video screens, suffer from low passenger engagement and retention. This project proposes a \textbf{Virtual Reality (VR) Airplane Safety Training System} to address this issue. By leveraging the \textbf{StereoKit} framework and \textbf{C\#}, we developed an immersive simulation that places the user inside a Boeing 737-800 cabin. The system utilizes a custom video playback engine for instruction and an interactive quiz module for verification. Preliminary testing suggests that the active participation required by VR significantly enhances user attention and understanding of critical safety procedures compared to passive observation.
